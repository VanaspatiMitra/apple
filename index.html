About the Crop:

Apple - McIntosh Red is a popular variety known for its sweet, tart flavor and crisp texture. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils with a pH of 6.0 to 6.8. The tree requires approximately 800-1,200 mm of water per acre annually, translating to about 2.62 to 3.94 feet. Typically, McIntosh Red yields around 8,000 to 12,000 kg of apples per acre. Profit margins can be favorable, ranging from 20-30%, depending on market prices, production costs, and yield.

  • Soil Composition: Well-drained, loamy soils with good aeration with PH 6.0 to 6.8.
  • Water need per Acre: 2.62 to 3.94 feet
  • Potential Dangers: Apple Maggot, Codling Moth, Pest Infestation, WaterStress
  • Fertilizers: Diammonium Phosphate, Calcium Nitrate, Organic Matter, Potassium, Nitrogen
  • Pesticides: Spinosad,Thiamethoxam, Acetamiprid
  • Profit Per Acre: ₹4,80,000 to ₹14,40,000
